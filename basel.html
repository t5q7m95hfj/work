Points of interest
Lyon → Mâcon
~75 km
RIGHT side of coach
Bresse region begins just east of the A6 — homeland of France's only AOC chicken. Flat plains, farming country.
Chalon-Nord → Beaune
~25 km
BOTH sides — vineyards
Route des Grands Crus. Côte de Beaune begins. Vineyard plots (climates) on the gentle slopes — some of the most valuable farmland on earth per square metre.
Dole
~60 km from Beaune
LEFT side
Louis Pasteur's birthplace. Town on the Doubs. Quick mention as you pass the exit sign.
Jura Mountains
visible ~70–120 km from Beaune
RIGHT side of coach
Mountains that gave the Jurassic period its name. Dinosaur-era limestone. Comté cheese and vin jaune come from up there. Good window moment — visible for a long stretch.
Besançon
~100 km from Beaune
LEFT — citadel visible from bypass
Victor Hugo's birthplace. UNESCO Vauban citadel sits dramatically on a loop of the Doubs river — visible from the A36 bypass if clear weather.
Montbéliard
~130 km from Beaune
LEFT side
Peugeot was founded here in 1810 — started making springs and saw blades. The factory is still here. Lion logo visible on some buildings near the exit.
Mulhouse
~160 km from Beaune
Pass-through
Home of the Cité de l'Automobile — world's largest car museum, 500+ vehicles including the best Bugatti collection anywhere. Mention it in passing.
Dreiländereck · Basel
destination
Rhine — AHEAD
Three-country corner: France, Germany, Switzerland meet at the Rhine. The Viking Hlin docks right at Westquaistrasse 75 — Swiss side. Remind pax to have passports accessible.

Section A — Yes or No · French Culture & Connections
1
Is Brie a type of French wine?
No — it's a cheese from the Brie region (Île-de-France).
ANS
2
Did Napoleon die in France?
No — he died in exile on Saint Helena island, 1821.
ANS
3
Is the Eiffel Tower taller than the Empire State Building?
No — Empire State Building is ~443m (to roof), Eiffel Tower ~330m.
ANS
4
Is French the second most spoken language in the world?
No — Spanish and Mandarin both rank above French. French is around 5th by native speakers.
ANS
5
Is "Française" the French word for "French woman"?
Yes — Française (f) / Français (m).
ANS
6
Is Paris the most visited city in the world?
It regularly ranks in the top 3, but Bangkok and London often compete for #1. Debatable — accept yes with discussion.
ANS
7
Is croissant originally from France?
No — it originates from Austria (the kipferl). Marie Antoinette is said to have brought it to France.
ANS
8
Did Coco Chanel invent high heels?
No — high heels date back to 16th-century Persia. Chanel actually popularised flat shoes for women.
ANS
9
Is Marseille on the Atlantic coast?
No — Mediterranean coast, south of France.
ANS
10
Did Edith Piaf sing "Non, je ne regrette rien"?
Yes — 1960, one of her most iconic recordings.
ANS
11
Is the Louvre the largest art museum in the world?
Yes — by floor area (72,735 m²), the Louvre is the world's largest art museum.
ANS
12
Did the French Revolution happen before the American Revolution?
No — American Revolution 1775–1783, French Revolution 1789–1799.
ANS

Which American jazz musician found adoration in Paris in the 20th century?

Who wrote and produced all the French "Taxi" movies?
Luc Besson (writer/producer). Directed by Gérard Krawczyk.

Which famous American popular artist is buried in Père Lachaise?

10
Why is a croissant called a croissant?
Croissant = "crescent" in French — named for its curved crescent moon shape.
