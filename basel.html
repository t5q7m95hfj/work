Arrival notes
Route schematic · Lyon → Basel
Lyon at bottom · Basel at top · travelling north then northeast. Left/right callouts are from inside the coach facing forward.
Leg-by-leg guide
Lyon → Mâcon
~75 km · A6
RIGHT side → Bresse plains · LEFT side → Saône valley
Flat agricultural corridor. Saône river visible on the left at times. East of the A6 begins Bresse — France's only AOC chicken territory (blue feet, white feathers, red comb). You'll also pass Villefranche-sur-Saône around the 30km mark — gateway to Beaujolais wine country.
Mâcon stop → Chalon-Nord exit
~25 km · A6
LEFT side → Côte d'Or slopes beginning
Terrain shifts. The gentle slopes to the left are the start of the Côte d'Or — some of the world's most expensive farmland per square metre. Tournus (where Patrick boarded from) is just before Mâcon. Exit Chalon-Nord to join the Route des Grands Crus — a narrow wine road through the villages.
Wine road → Beaune
~20 km · D974
BOTH sides → famous vineyard villages
Passing through: Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet (world's finest whites), Pommard, Volnay, Nuits-Saint-Georges. Each village sign is a wine label you'd pay €30+ for in a restaurant. Hospices de Beaune visible as you enter town.
Beaune → Dole
~60 km · A36
RIGHT side → Jura ridge appearing · LEFT → Doubs valley
Route bends northeast on the A36. The Doubs river starts appearing on the left. The Jura ridge begins rising on the right — limestone mountains, folded by the same ancient collision that built the Alps. Dole exit sign on the left — Pasteur's birthplace, good moment to mention.
Dole → Besançon
~40 km · A36
RIGHT → Jura peaks clearly visible · LEFT → Doubs bends
Best Jura views in this stretch — the ridge is close and dramatic. The road follows the Doubs valley. Poligny and Arbois are in the Jura to the right — where vin jaune (yellow wine) is made, aged for 6 years minimum under a layer of yeast. Besançon citadel appears dramatically on a hill surrounded by the Doubs loop.
Besançon stop
Aire d'Écot · 20 min
LEFT → Vauban citadel on hill · UNESCO listed
Victor Hugo born here 1802. The Vauban citadel (17th century) sits on a rocky spur almost entirely encircled by the river Doubs — visible from the bypass on a clear day. Remind pax ~1h45 to Basel from here.
Besançon → Montbéliard
~45 km · A36
RIGHT → Jura still visible · LEFT → widening plain
Terrain opens out as the Jura begins to recede. Montbéliard exit on the left — Peugeot founded here in 1810 (started with clock springs and saw blades before pivoting to bikes then cars). The factory complex is still visible near the road. The town has an Alsatian feel — Protestant, historically a small German-speaking principality until 1793.
Montbéliard → Mulhouse
~40 km · A36
LEFT → Vosges range appearing · RIGHT → Jura fading
The Trouée de Belfort (Belfort Gap) — a flat corridor between the Jura to the south and the Vosges mountains to the north. Historically the only easy passage between the Rhine plain and France — armies, trade, and railways all used it. Alsace begins here. Architecture shifts: timber-frame houses, storks (sometimes on rooftops). Mulhouse signposted — Cité de l'Automobile, world's largest car museum (500+ vehicles, best Bugatti collection anywhere).
Mulhouse → Basel
~35 km · A35/A2
AHEAD → Rhine · Switzerland
Final approach. Route passes Saint-Louis — last French town before the border. The Rhine appears ahead, wide and slow. Dreiländereck: France, Germany, Switzerland meet at a single point on the river. Viking Hlin docks on the Swiss bank at Westquaistrasse 75. Passports accessible — Swiss customs may board.
